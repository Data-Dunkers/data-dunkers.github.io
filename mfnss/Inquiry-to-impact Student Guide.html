Inquiry‑to‑Impact

Explore a real‑world issue, build understanding from reliable sources, and create something that helps others learn — while using the internet responsibly.

Phase 1 — Find What Matters

KC: real‑world issues
DC: responsible participation

What this phase is about: Choosing a real‑world topic or problem that connects to your life, community, or the world. No question yet — just the topic.

Questions to ask yourself
  • What’s happening around me that I care about or want to understand?
  • Why does this issue or idea matter? Who is affected, and how?
  • What do I already know? What do I hope could change or improve?

Phase 2 — Ask a Big Question

KC: plan research with questions

What this phase is about: Turning your topic into one strong, open‑ended question that will guide your research.

Questions to ask yourself
  • What do I really want to figure out or explain to others?
  • Can my question start with how, why, or what happens when?
  • Is my question specific enough to research, but big enough to explore?

Phase 3 — Dig for Clues

KC: evaluate & curate sources
DC: credibility, attribution

What this phase is about: Gathering trustworthy information to help answer your Big Question — and keeping track of where it came from.

Questions to ask yourself
  • Who created this source, and why?
  • Is the information recent, accurate, and backed by evidence?
  • What bias or missing voice could affect this?
  • Where else can I check to confirm what I found?

Phase 4 — Make Sense of It

KC: build knowledge
Apply learning

What this phase is about: Organizing what you found, spotting patterns, and forming your own understanding.

Questions to ask yourself
  • What ideas or facts show up again and again?
  • How do different sources agree or disagree?
  • What seems most important or surprising?
  • What do I now understand that I didn’t before?

Phase 5 — Create Something That Helps Others

KC: communicate understanding
Product & evidence

What this phase is about: Using what you learned to teach, inform, or improve something for others.

Questions to ask yourself
  • What product will best share my learning (poster, video, slides, infographic, article, PSA)?
  • What evidence or examples will I include?
  • How can I make it clear, interesting, and accurate?

Phase 6 — Share, Credit & Reflect

DC: ethical sharing & credit

What this phase is about: Sharing your work responsibly and looking back on your learning.

Questions to ask yourself
  • Who should see my work (classmates, families, community)?
  • Did I give credit for all images, facts, and quotes I used?
  • Did I represent other people’s ideas fairly?
  • What was most interesting or challenging? How did my thinking change?
  • What will I explore next?

Quick Tips

  • Cross‑check facts with at least two sources.
  • Save the link the moment you find something useful.
  • Use your own words when explaining ideas.
  • Give credit for images, quotes, and ideas you did not create.
