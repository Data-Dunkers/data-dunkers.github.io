Instructional Planning

How are teachers using AI for instructional planning?

  • Co-planning unit/lesson sequences from standards and outcomes
  • Drafting lesson flows and timing across launch → practice → application
  • Surfacing likely misconceptions and planning checkpoints
  • Generating examples, non-examples, and discussion questions
  • Creating parallel lessons for different sections/preps

Things to consider

  • Begin with outcomes and cognitive demand; let tools follow the goal
  • Keep alignment to standards visible and intentional
  • Plan how students will show thinking, not just answers
  • Build time for iteration and for students to critique AI ideas
  • Anticipate misconceptions; place checks where they matter

Tips when creating with MagicSchoolAI

  • Use Lesson Planner/Lesson Improver + Standard Aligner; ask for rationale for each step
  • Prompt for “misconceptions to anticipate” and “checks for understanding”
  • Request multiple versions: inquiry, workshop, gradual release
  • Generate teacher prompts and student stems, then trim to your voice

Developing Materials, Scaffolds, and Supports

How are teachers using AI for materials and supports?

  • Producing leveled readings, glossaries, and sentence frames
  • Turning dense text into step-by-step guides while keeping vocabulary
  • Building graphic organizers and note guides that fade over time
  • Creating mentor examples and practice sets with feedback keys

Things to consider

  • Remove barriers without lowering rigor
  • Design scaffolds to fade (model → guided → independent)
  • Apply UDL: multiple ways to access, engage, and express
  • Check linguistic load and concept accuracy on every output

Tips when creating with MagicSchoolAI

  • Use Differentiation Wizard, Modify Materials, Simplify Language
  • Prompt: “Simplify sentence structure, keep academic vocabulary intact”
  • Ask for “a scaffold that fades across three steps”
  • Request paired examples & non-examples tied to the concept

Developing Assessments and Assignments

How are teachers using AI for assessments and assignments?

  • Converting recall tasks into analysis/design prompts
  • Generating performance tasks with authentic audiences
  • Drafting rubrics that include reasoning/process criteria
  • Creating reflection prompts and evidence checklists

Things to consider

  • Assess reasoning and decision-making, not just final products
  • Triangulate evidence (product, process artifacts, brief oral checks)
  • Require drafts, notes, and prompt logs to make thinking visible
  • Avoid generic prompts easily answered by AI without context

Tips when creating with MagicSchoolAI

  • Use Assessment Generator / Rubric Creator; add “process evidence” criteria
  • Use Critical Thinking Task Generator to lift cognitive level
  • Prompt: “Redesign to value originality, justification, and tradeoffs”
  • Ask for short oral defense questions aligned to the task

When Differentiating

How are teachers using AI for differentiation?

  • Creating tiered tasks aimed at the same learning goal
  • Designing station rotations by readiness/interest/profile
  • Generating multiple modalities for access (text, visuals, outlines)
  • Producing enrichment/extension pathways efficiently

Things to consider

  • Differentiate content/process/product—keep expectations high
  • Offer equivalently rigorous options with student choice
  • Attend to linguistic, cultural, and neurodiverse needs
  • Build differentiation into initial planning, not after the fact

Tips when creating with MagicSchoolAI

  • Use Differentiation Wizard with explicit parameters
  • Prompt: “Maintain conceptual rigor while scaffolding language/access”
  • Use Learning Station Generator for 3–4 purposeful stations
  • Ask for multimodal outputs (diagram + short text + quick-check)

Developing AI Literacy

How are teachers using AI for AI literacy?

  • Embedding short critique tasks inside content lessons
  • Modeling disclosure of how/why AI was used
  • Using decision frameworks for when to use AI vs. not
  • Structuring reflections on reliability, improvement, and ethics

Things to consider

  • Integrate literacy within core content, not as a stand-alone
  • Emphasize transparency, critique, and responsible decisions
  • Compare AI outputs to sources/experiments/class notes
  • Keep reflections brief and frequent to build habits

Tips when creating with MagicSchoolAI

  • Use AI Literacy Lesson Generator / Digital Citizenship Coach
  • Prompt: “Create a 10-minute mini-task to critique an AI output in this topic”
  • Use Prompt Reflection Designer to build a student disclosure log
  • Ask for a grade-level decision guide (e.g., AI stoplight)
